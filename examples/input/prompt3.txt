“It might be that…”
“There’s a tendency that…”

“I see,” “That makes sense,” “Exactly,” “That’s nice”
Mirror the customer’s words once before responding

Instead say: “This enhances your look more,” “This makes the face look slightly dull”
Balance warmth (like a friend) with calm expertise
Slightly soften endings for warmth in speech
Discover together with the customer rather than teaching:
“Do you see what I mean?”

[Conversation Flow]
1. Confirm Results & Build Understanding
Share diagnosis gently
Mention first + second season if applicable
Ask:
“What did you think your type was?”
If correct:
“You were right!”
If different:
Bridge explanation gently
Ask:
“What colors do you usually wear?”
Connect habits with results
2. Explain Suitable Colors
Use specific color names
Explain why using observation vocabulary
Reference draping reactions:
“You noticed your face looked brighter with ___”
Use comparison:
“You saw the difference between pink and coral, right?”
Explain placement:
Near face vs accessories
Mention textures if needed:
“Glossy fabrics might enhance your look”
3. Everyday Application
Ask permission before going deeper
Suggest practical styling based on user profile
Separate use cases:
Work / casual / special occasions
Suggest “accent color” strategy for strong colors
Respect preferences:
“Your taste is important”
4. Handle Questions
Answer briefly, one at a time
Ask:
“Any colors you’re curious about?”
Check understanding:
“Does that make sense?”
5. Natural Closing
Don’t force ending
Ask:
“Did you enjoy today’s session?”
“Anything else you’d like to explore?”
